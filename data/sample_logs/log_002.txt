Research Log — Thermostable Enzyme Variants
Date: 2025-01-21
Researcher: Dr. B. Patel
Team: Biology

Objective:
Characterize thermostable beta-galactosidase variants from Bacillus subtilis and compare activity profiles to commercial lactase under elevated temperature conditions.

Methods:
- Three B. subtilis variants (BsLac-A, BsLac-B, BsLac-C) expressed in E. coli BL21.
- Purified via Ni-NTA affinity chromatography; purity confirmed by SDS-PAGE.
- Activity assays at 37°C, 50°C, 65°C, and 80°C using ONPG substrate.
- Half-life (t1/2) measured at each temperature by tracking residual activity over 2 hours.
- Km and Vmax determined under optimal temperature per variant.

Results:
- BsLac-C showed highest thermostability: t1/2 = 82 min at 65°C vs. 8 min for commercial lactase.
- BsLac-C maintained 91% activity at 50°C compared to commercial lactase at 38%.
- Km for BsLac-C at 37°C = 0.52 mM (comparable to commercial enzyme at 0.48 mM).
- Vmax for BsLac-C at optimal 55°C = 198 µmol/min/mg — 39% higher than commercial at 37°C.
- BsLac-A and BsLac-B showed intermediate stability, not suitable for industrial processes.

Observations:
- Disulfide bridge at Cys-142 in BsLac-C correlates with enhanced thermal resistance.
- Circular dichroism shows retention of secondary structure up to 70°C for BsLac-C.
- All three variants show similar pH optima (6.5–7.5), suitable for gut-environment applications.

Next Steps:
- Test BsLac-C in simulated dairy processing conditions (72°C pasteurization).
- Initiate patent filing for BsLac-C industrial formulation.
- Cross-reference findings with Dr. Chen's kinetics study for comparative publication.
