Research Log — Enzyme Kinetics Study
Date: 2025-01-14
Researcher: Dr. A. Chen
Team: Biology

Objective:
Investigate the effect of temperature on the catalytic efficiency of lactase enzyme using ONPG as substrate.

Methods:
- Prepared 0.1 M phosphate buffer at pH 7.0.
- Lactase (5 U/mL) incubated with ONPG (2 mM) at 25°C, 37°C, and 50°C.
- Absorbance measured at 420 nm every 30 seconds over 10 minutes.
- Vmax and Km calculated from Michaelis-Menten plots using GraphPad Prism.

Results:
- At 37°C, Vmax = 142 µmol/min/mg, Km = 0.48 mM (highest efficiency).
- At 25°C, Vmax = 89 µmol/min/mg, Km = 0.61 mM.
- At 50°C, Vmax dropped to 54 µmol/min/mg — enzyme denaturation observed above 45°C.
- Activation energy calculated at 48 kJ/mol between 25–37°C.
- Temperature coefficient (Q10) = 1.8 in physiological range.

Observations:
- Enzyme showed classic bell-shaped activity curve with peak at 37°C.
- SDS-PAGE confirmed protein aggregation at 50°C.
- No significant change in Km at higher temperatures suggests non-competitive thermal inhibition.

Next Steps:
- Test thermostable lactase variants from Bacillus subtilis for comparison.
- Conduct fluorescence quenching assays to confirm denaturation mechanism.
- Submit findings to Journal of Biochemical Kinetics by Q2 2025.
